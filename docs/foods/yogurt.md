## Overview

Yogurt is fermented dairy providing live probiotics, complete protein, and B vitamins, supporting gut-brain axis communication.

## Key Substances

### Nutrients
- **Protein**: Complete amino acid profile
- **Calcium**: High content
- **B Vitamins**: Produced during fermentation

### Bioactive Substances
- **Probiotics**: Live beneficial bacteria (varies by product)
- **Postbiotics**: Beneficial fermentation compounds

## From the paper

- "Fermented dairy" mentioned as nutrient-dense animal food
- "Practical pairings: spinach + eggs, tomatoes + olive oil, salad + oil, carrots + tahini, kale + avocado, berries + yogurt/nuts"
- "Cultured dairy: Yogurt, kefir, buttermilk (if tolerated)"

## Preparation Notes

- Choose products with live active cultures
- Part of fermented foods strategy
- Supports gut microbiome diversity
- Pair with berries/nuts for polyphenol synergy
