## Overview

Corn provides carotenoids (lutein, zeaxanthin) and complex carbohydrates, supporting eye/brain health and stable glucose release.

## Key Substances

### Nutrients

- **Carotenoids**: Lutein and zeaxanthin
- **Complex carbohydrates**: Starch
- **Fiber**: Some fiber content

### Bioactive Substances

- **Lutein/Zeaxanthin**: Carotenoids for eye and brain health

## From the paper

- "Carotenoids are abundant in leafy greens, orange and yellow vegetables, corn, and egg yolks"

## Preparation Notes

- Pair with fat for carotenoid absorption
- Can be consumed fresh, cooked, or as whole grain
- Part of diverse vegetable intake
- Supports carotenoid diversity
