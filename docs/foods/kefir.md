## Overview

Kefir is fermented milk providing diverse probiotics, including potential GABA-producing strains, and postbiotic compounds.

## Key Substances

### Nutrients
- **Protein**: Complete amino acid profile
- **Calcium**: High content
- **B Vitamins**: Produced during fermentation

### Bioactive Substances
- **Probiotics**: Diverse strains including potential GABA producers
- **Postbiotics**: Beneficial fermentation compounds

## From the paper

- "Fermented Foods: Sauerkraut, kimchi, kefir, miso, kombucha - Live microbes + postbiotic peptides; improved SCFA pools; vagal signaling"
- "Kefir: Fermented milk drink, diverse probiotic strains"
- "Levilactobacillus brevis... This bacteria is active in L. brevis strains isolated from fermented foods, such as kimchi, kefir, and pickles"
- "Nutrient density is a central premise... kimchi and kefir give microbiome support"

## Preparation Notes

- Choose products with live active cultures
- Part of fermented foods rotation
- Supports gut microbiome diversity
- Potential GABA production via specific strains
