## Overview

Parmesan cheese provides CLA (conjugated linoleic acid), vitamin K2, C15:0 pentadecanoic acid, high protein, and calcium, with studies showing it doesn't raise LDL despite high calcium.

## Key Substances

### Nutrients
- **Protein**: High protein content
- **Calcium**: High content (doesn't raise LDL)
- **Vitamin K2**: Important for bone and cardiovascular health

### Bioactive Substances
- **CLA (Conjugated Linoleic Acid)**: Rumenic acid and other forms
- **C15:0 (Pentadecanoic Acid)**: Emerging research on health benefits
- **Glutamate**: Umami flavor compound

## From the paper

- "Parmesan Cheese: CLA, vitamin K2, glutamate, high protein, calcium; fermented, C15"
- "C15:0 (Parmesan and grassfed Butter)"
- "Studies have shown that hard cheeses with high calcium do not raise serum LDL levels"
- "Instead of excluding them, dietary strategies should focus on unprocessed, nutrient-dense sources such as Parmesan cheese, grass-fed butter, and pasture-raised egg yolks"

## Preparation Notes

- Use as grated topping or snacks
- Part of nutrient-dense animal food strategy
- Supports K2 and calcium intake
- Choose quality sources when possible
