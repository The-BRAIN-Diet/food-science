## Overview

Kale is a nutrient-dense leafy green providing iron, magnesium, zinc, quercetin, and carotenoids, though oxalates can bind minerals.

## Key Substances

### Nutrients
- **Iron**: Non-heme iron (enhance with vitamin C)
- **Magnesium**: Cofactor for neurotransmitter pathways
- **Zinc**: Plant source (improve bioavailability via preparation)
- **Folate (B9)**: Methylation support
- **Vitamin K**: High content
- **Vitamin A**: From beta-carotene

### Bioactive Substances
- **Quercetin**: Flavonoid polyphenol
- **Carotenoids**: Lutein, zeaxanthin, beta-carotene
- **Oxalates**: Can bind minerals; reduce via boiling

- "Boiling spinach, kale, and other greens can reduce oxalate load, improving mineral bioavailability"

## Preparation Notes

- Boiling reduces oxalates and improves mineral bioavailability
- Pair with fat (avocado, olive oil) for carotenoid absorption
- Pair with vitamin C sources to enhance iron absorption
- Part of diverse leafy green strategy
