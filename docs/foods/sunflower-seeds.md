## Overview

Sunflower seeds provide thiamine (B1), vitamin E, and are the source of sunflower lecithin, supporting mitochondrial function and choline intake.

## Key Substances

### Nutrients
- **Thiamine (B1)**: Essential for mitochondrial glucose metabolism
- **Vitamin E**: Antioxidant
- **Protein**: Plant protein
- **Selenium**: Some content

## From the paper

- "Thiamine (B1): Pork, sunflower seeds, salmon, peas, rice, lentils"
- "Vegans should ensure adequate choline intake (e.g., soy or sunflower lecithin, soy foods, quinoa, broccoli)"

## Preparation Notes

- Can be consumed raw or roasted
- Part of diverse seed intake
- Supports mitochondrial function via thiamine
- Source of sunflower lecithin
