### BRS6(KC1) - Glucose / Energy Substrate Sufficiency

### 1. Ambition

Maintain stable meal-derived energy-substrate sufficiency so glucose continuity and glycaemic regulation support neuroendocrine allocation without excessive volatility.

### 2. Shared Biological Pool

- Slow-release carbohydrate substrates ← oats, barley, legumes
- Dietary protein substrate context ← fish, eggs, dairy, legumes
- Dietary fat substrate context ← olive oil, nuts, seeds, fish
- Soluble-viscous fibre classes ← oats, barley, pulses, apples

### 3. Biological Importance

Neuronal activity requires tightly regulated cerebral glucose metabolism coordinated with energy demand [Mergenthaler et al., 2013]. Dietary carbohydrate quality and fibre-rich patterns influence post-prandial glycaemic load and variability at the population level [Reynolds et al., 2019]. When substrate delivery is erratic or marked by large glycaemic excursions, systems that depend on predictable fuel supply face greater physiological strain [Reynolds et al., 2019; Mergenthaler et al., 2013].
