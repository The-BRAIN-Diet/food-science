### BRS6(KC2) - Stress-Response Micronutrient & Lipid Sufficiency

### 1. Ambition

Maintain foundational micronutrient and membrane-lipid sufficiency so stress-response enzyme systems, autonomic regulation, and recovery capacity remain supported.

### 2. Shared Biological Pool

- Magnesium ← leafy greens, nuts, seeds
- Vitamin C ← citrus, kiwi, peppers
- B vitamins (B1, B2, B3, B5, B6, B7, B9, B12) ← whole grains, legumes, eggs
- Iron and zinc ← seafood, meat, legumes, seeds
- Long-chain omega-3 fatty acids (EPA, DHA) ← oily fish, algae

### 3. Biological Importance

Vitamins and minerals are required for core pathways in energy metabolism, oxygen handling, and neuronal function [Tardy et al., 2020]. B vitamins act as co-enzymes across brain energy production, methylation, and neurochemical synthesis [Kennedy, 2016]. Long-chain omega-3 fatty acids are structural and signalling components of brain lipids [McNamara & Carlson, 2006]. Insufficiency across these nutrient classes impairs recovery capacity under physiological load [Tardy et al., 2020; McNamara & Carlson, 2006].
