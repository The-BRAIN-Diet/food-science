## BRS6(FM2) - HPA Axis Rhythm & Cortisol Regulation

## 1. Definition

Integrated regulation of cortisol rhythm and light–feeding entrainment across waking, feeding, and recovery cycles, influencing stress-hormone amplitude, phase alignment, and diurnal neuroendocrine stability.

## 2. Intervention Breakdown

Behavioural/Lifestyle Dominant

## 3. Timing Specific

Yes

## 4. Functional Role

↑ cortisol rhythm stability; ↑ morning activation; ↓ evening stress-hormone drift; ↑ circadian phase alignment

## 5. Mechanistic Basis (Implementation of PMs)

BRS6(FM2) is implemented through BRS6(PM4) and BRS6(PM5): PM4 governs diurnal cortisol amplitude and phase, especially morning activation and evening downshift; PM5 governs alignment of feeding windows, light exposure, and sleep timing with circadian metabolic and neuroendocrine rhythms. Together they operationalise FM2 as coordinated timing control of stress-hormone expression and circadian entrainment, translating daily structure into steadier cortisol rhythm and reduced evening neuroendocrine drift.

## 7. Dietary Levers

<details>
<summary><strong>Diet</strong></summary>

- Regular protein-rich breakfast → morning energy and catecholamine support within the waking phase.
- Consistent meal timing with early-day energy loading → circadian metabolic entrainment.
- Reduced late-night eating → lower circadian misalignment pressure on evening cortisol.

</details>

## 8. Lifestyle Levers

<details>
<summary><strong>Lifestyle</strong></summary>

- Morning daylight exposure → circadian alignment and cortisol phase support.
- Reduced evening light and stable sleep–wake timing → improved cortisol amplitude and phase alignment.
- Stress regulation practices → reduced evening cortisol spillover.

</details>
