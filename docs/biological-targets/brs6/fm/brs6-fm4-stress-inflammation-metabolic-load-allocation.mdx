## BRS6(FM4) - Stress-Inflammation / Metabolic Load Allocation

## 1. Definition

Integrated regulation of metabolic-inflammatory load and stress-linked appetite–reward signalling, influencing whole-body resource allocation and brain-relevant energy/stress state.

## 2. Functional Role

↓ metabolic stress load; ↓ stress-driven appetite volatility; ↑ stable energy allocation; ↓ chronic inflammatory pressure on neuroendocrine allocation

## 3. Mechanistic Basis (Implementation of PMs)

BRS6(FM4) is implemented through BRS6(PM8) and BRS6(PM9): PM8 governs inflammatory and endocrine signalling from metabolic overload, adipose stress, and insulin-resistant states; PM9 governs stress-related modulation of appetite, reward drive, and food-seeking behaviour. Together they operationalise FM4 as coordinated allocation of metabolic and neuroendocrine resources under chronic stress, translating dietary pattern and stress exposure into steadier energy allocation and reduced stress-driven appetite volatility.

## 5. Dietary Levers

<details>
<summary><strong>Diet</strong></summary>

- Anti-inflammatory Mediterranean-style pattern → reduced inflammatory load.
- Fibre-rich meals → improved metabolic signalling context.
- Protein-rich breakfast and structured meal composition → improved appetite and reward stability.

</details>

## 6. Lifestyle Levers

<details>
<summary><strong>Lifestyle</strong></summary>

- Stress regulation practices → reduced cortisol-driven eating pressure.
- Regular physical activity and recovery balance → improved insulin sensitivity and inflammatory resolution.
- Sleep timing and duration stability → lower allostatic metabolic load.

</details>
