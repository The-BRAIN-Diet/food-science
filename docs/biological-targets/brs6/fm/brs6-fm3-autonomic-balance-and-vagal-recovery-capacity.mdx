## BRS6(FM3) - Autonomic Balance & Vagal Recovery Capacity

## 1. Definition

Integrated regulation of sympathetic–parasympathetic balance and vagal recovery capacity after stress or cognitive demand, influencing autonomic flexibility, HRV context, and physiological downshifting.

## 2. Functional Role

↑ vagal recovery; ↑ HRV context; ↓ chronic sympathetic load; ↑ autonomic flexibility after demand

## 3. Mechanistic Basis (Implementation of PMs)

BRS6(FM3) is implemented through BRS6(PM6) and BRS6(PM7): PM6 governs sympathetic arousal and the shift back into parasympathetic recovery after stress, exercise, or cognitive demand; PM7 governs vagal tone and heart-rate-variability-related recovery signalling. Together they operationalise FM3 as coordinated autonomic downshifting and recovery capacity, translating stress load and recovery inputs into restored parasympathetic flexibility and reduced persistent sympathetic tone.

## 5. Dietary Levers

<details>
<summary><strong>Diet</strong></summary>

- Omega-3-rich foods → HRV and vagal recovery support context.
- Magnesium-rich foods → neuromuscular relaxation support.
- Fermented foods plus fibre → gut–vagal signalling support.
- Stable meal composition → reduced sympathetic-reactive swings.

</details>

## 6. Lifestyle Levers

<details>
<summary><strong>Lifestyle</strong></summary>

- Slow breathing and vagal training practices → increased parasympathetic tone.
- Recovery-focused exercise dosing → improved autonomic flexibility.
- Sleep continuity and stress downshifting routines → reduced chronic sympathetic load.

</details>
