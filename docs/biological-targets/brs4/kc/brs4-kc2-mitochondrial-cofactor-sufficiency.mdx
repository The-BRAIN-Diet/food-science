### BRS4(KC2) - Mitochondrial Cofactor Sufficiency

(Vitamins & Minerals That Power Mitochondria)

### 1. Ambition

Maintain mitochondrial cofactor sufficiency so enzyme systems can sustain electron transfer, redox stability, and ATP-generating efficiency.

### 2. Core Nutritional Requirements

- B vitamins (B1, B2, B3, B5, B6, B7, B9, B12) ← whole grains, legumes, eggs
- Iron ← meat, shellfish, legumes
- Magnesium ← leafy greens, nuts, seeds
- CoQ10-supportive lipid context ← oily fish, meat, eggs

### 3. Evidence Base

#### Summary

Connected BRS4 mechanisms share a second upstream nutritional condition alongside macronutrient fuels: the diet must supply **B-vitamin, iron, magnesium, and CoQ10-supportive lipid context** sufficient for mitochondrial enzyme function, electron transfer, and redox stability. When cofactors are insufficient, available fuel substrates are processed less efficiently — limiting cellular energy output across FM1, FM2, FM3, and FM4 PMs in parallel.

Practical framing: maintain **B-vitamin-rich whole foods**, **iron and magnesium sources**, and **lipid contexts supporting CoQ10 biology** across daily meals; recognise that ETC function, NAD⁺ metabolism, and mitochondrial protection all depend on cofactor sufficiency rather than fuel alone. The evidence claim here is **constraint prevention** — adequate availability is required to prevent biological constraint — not that additional intake enhances performance in nutrient-sufficient people.

#### Biological Importance

B vitamins function as coenzymes across mitochondrial energy metabolism — including NAD⁺-linked redox chemistry, acetyl-CoA handling, and electron-transfer reactions represented across FM1 and FM2 PMs. Dietary B-vitamin sufficiency is therefore a shared enabling condition for converting macronutrient substrates into usable ATP.

#### Biological Importance

Iron supports cytochrome-mediated electron transfer and oxygen-handling chemistry, while magnesium is required for ATP stabilisation and multiple mitochondrial enzymatic steps. Together they represent mineral cofactor classes whose insufficiency can limit ETC throughput and energetic resilience even when macronutrient fuels are present.

#### Biological Importance

Coenzyme Q10 is an electron carrier embedded in mitochondrial membranes whose endogenous status depends partly on dietary lipid context and whole-food matrix supplying membrane lipids and synthesis precursors. This KC names lipid-context sufficiency rather than CoQ10 supplementation as the shared dietary requirement supporting FM1 ETC and FM2 redox-integrity mechanisms.

### 4. Emerging Biological Supports

No Emerging Biological Supports are currently prioritised for this KC.

CoQ10 supplementation, NAD⁺ precursors, or other mitochondrial adjuncts may support related bioenergetic capacities under specific conditions, but they are not established as shared indispensable dietary requirements for mitochondrial cofactor sufficiency. Where evidence becomes source-led and KC-specific, candidates can be added here without blurring the Core Nutritional Requirements boundary.
