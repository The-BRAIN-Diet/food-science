## BRS4(PM4) - NAD⁺ Metabolism

## 1. Definition

NAD⁺ availability for redox reactions, oxidative metabolism, and mitochondrial signalling.

This PM captures the redox-carrier economy that helps connect nutrient sufficiency to mitochondrial metabolic throughput within [BRS4(FM1) - Cellular Bioenergetics](/docs/biological-targets/brs4/fm/brs4-fm1-cellular-bioenergetics) [1][2][3].

## 2. Intervention Breakdown

Food-State Leaning

## 3. Functional Role

Improved redox reactions; improved mitochondrial metabolic capacity

## 4. Mechanistic Basis

### Summary

BRS4(PM4) links niacin status, whole-diet adequacy, and broader metabolic context to mitochondrial redox handling and oxidative metabolism [1][2][3].

<details>
<summary><strong>NAD⁺ availability and mitochondrial metabolism</strong></summary>

#### (Redox-carrying role)

NAD⁺ and related redox carriers are central to mitochondrial oxidative metabolism, helping channel nutrient-derived electrons into ATP-generating processes.

#### (Dietary support context)

Niacin-rich foods and protein-containing whole foods contribute precursor context, while overall dietary sufficiency influences whether NAD⁺-dependent metabolic reactions can proceed efficiently [1][2].

#### (Cross-BRS context)

Because one-carbon and broader methylation-related metabolism intersect with redox handling, this PM links outward to [BRS2(PM4) - Methionine Cycle Flux](/docs/biological-targets/brs2/pm/brs2-pm4-methionine-cycle-flux).

</details>
