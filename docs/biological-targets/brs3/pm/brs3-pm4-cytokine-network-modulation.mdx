## BRS3(PM4) - Cytokine Network Modulation

## 1. Definition

Modulation of cytokine signalling tone across IL-6, TNF-a, CRP-linked inflammatory pathways.

This PM governs downstream cytokine-signalling tone rather than upstream transcriptional nodes or gut-derived endotoxin load.

## 2. Intervention Breakdown

Food-State Dominant

## 3. Functional Role

↓ IL-6/TNF-a pressure; ↓ inflammatory signalling tone

## 4. Mechanistic Basis

### Summary

Inflammatory tone is expressed not only at transcriptional nodes but through circulating cytokine networks. This PM captures whether IL-6, TNF-α, and CRP-linked signalling remains elevated or moves toward resolution within [BRS3(FM3) - Inflammation Resolution Capacity](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/fm/brs3-fm3-inflammation-resolution-capacity).

<details>
<summary><strong>Cytokine tone and inflammatory-state modulation</strong></summary>

#### (Cytokine signalling burden)

IL-6, TNF-α, and CRP-linked pathways act as downstream readouts of inflammatory tone, reflecting how unresolved immune signalling is expressed systemically.

#### (Network modulation logic)

Cytokine pressure integrates upstream inputs from transcriptional, gut-derived, and lipid-mediator pathways. Modulation here reflects whether inflammatory signalling remains escalated or is progressively brought down—not a single receptor or cytokine in isolation.

#### (Boundaries of the mechanism)

Upstream NF-κB transcriptional control is handled by [BRS3(PM1) - NF-kB Signalling Regulation](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/pm/brs3-pm1-nf-kb-signalling-regulation). Gut-derived endotoxin signalling belongs to [BRS3(PM7) - Gut-Derived Inflammatory Signalling](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/pm/brs3-pm7-gut-derived-inflammatory-signalling). Lipid-mediator resolution substrate balance is represented by [BRS3(PM6) - Eicosanoid / SPM Balance](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/pm/brs3-pm6-eicosanoid-spm-balance).

#### (Integration within BRS3)

This PM supports resolution-oriented inflammatory tone within [BRS3(FM3)](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/fm/brs3-fm3-inflammation-resolution-capacity), drawing on [BRS3(KC3) - Essential Fatty Acid Balance](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/kc/brs3-kc3-essential-fatty-acid-balance). Stable glycaemic and gut context imported through section 5.3 cross-BRS links conditions downstream cytokine expression.

</details>

### 4.1 Evidence Highlights

#### Introduction/Summary

Cytokine networks are standard inflammatory readouts. The studies below highlight dietary-intervention findings that refine how cytokine-tone modulation is interpreted in practice.

<details>
<summary><strong>Evidence highlights — cytokine tone and diet</strong></summary>

- Omega-3 fatty acid interventions show measurable effects on inflammatory biomarkers including cytokine-linked pathways → [Ferguson et al. (2014)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#ferguson_omega3_2014) [2]
- Population and dietary-pattern associations link habitual intake patterns to IL-6, TNF-α, and CRP-linked inflammatory tone → [Brown et al. (2025)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#brown_associations_2025) [3]
- Stress–diet interactions illustrate that cytokine readouts shift with combined metabolic and behavioural context, supporting pattern-based rather than single-nutrient framing → [Chang et al. (2020)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#chang_cortisol_2020) [1]

</details>
