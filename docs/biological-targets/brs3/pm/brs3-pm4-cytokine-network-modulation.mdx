## BRS3(PM4) - Cytokine Network Modulation

## 1. Definition

Modulation of cytokine signalling tone across IL-6, TNF-a, CRP-linked inflammatory pathways.

This PM sits downstream of multiple inflammatory inputs and reflects how diet may shift signalling tone across biomarker-linked inflammatory networks relevant to BRS3 [1][2][3].

## 2. Intervention Breakdown

Food-State Dominant

## 3. Functional Role

↓ IL-6/TNF-a pressure; ↓ inflammatory signalling tone

## 4. Mechanistic Basis

### Summary

BRS3(PM4) links omega-3, polyphenol-rich, and gut-supportive dietary patterns to lower cytokine pressure within [BRS3(FM3) - Inflammation Resolution Capacity](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/fm/brs3-fm3-inflammation-resolution-capacity) [1][2][3].

<details>
<summary><strong>Cytokine tone and inflammatory-state modulation</strong></summary>

#### (Cytokine signalling burden)

IL-6, TNF-a, CRP-linked pathways act as readouts of inflammatory tone and help capture how unresolved inflammatory signalling is expressed downstream.

#### (Dietary modulation context)

Omega-3 intake, polyphenol-rich foods, and lower-LPS meal contexts may reduce cytokine pressure over time, especially when supported by stable glycaemic and gut inputs [2][3].

#### (FM placement)

Although cytokine modulation overlaps with inflammatory signalling tone more broadly, here it is placed under the resolution-oriented cluster because it helps define whether inflammatory activity remains escalated or is progressively brought down.

</details>
