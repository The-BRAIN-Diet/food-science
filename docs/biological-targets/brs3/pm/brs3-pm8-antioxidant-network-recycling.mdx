## BRS3(PM8) - Antioxidant Network Recycling

## 1. Definition

Recycling interactions among antioxidant systems that regenerate vitamin and thiol antioxidant capacity.

This PM governs antioxidant-network recycling and regeneration rather than single-compound antioxidant supply or transcriptional Nrf2 induction.

## 2. Intervention Breakdown

Food-State Dominant

## 3. Functional Role

↑ antioxidant regeneration; ↑ persistence of antioxidant protection

## 4. Mechanistic Basis

### Summary

Antioxidant protection persists only when depleted antioxidants are regenerated across interacting systems. Network recycling—not isolated nutrient presence—determines whether redox defence remains effective within [BRS3(FM2) - Antioxidant Defense Capacity](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/fm/brs3-fm2-antioxidant-defense-capacity).

<details>
<summary><strong>Antioxidant-network logic and recycling capacity</strong></summary>

#### (Network rather than single-nutrient protection)

Antioxidant protection functions as a network in which vitamin C, vitamin E, glutathione, CoQ10, lipoic acid, and related systems regenerate one another in vivo rather than acting as isolated nutrients → [Packer et al. (1997)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#packer_vitamin_1997) [1]; [Vertuani et al. (2004)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#vertuani_antioxidants_2004) [2]

#### (Regeneration cycles and cofactor dependence)

Recycling capacity depends on simultaneous availability of water-soluble and lipid-soluble antioxidants plus thiol and cofactor support. Depletion in one network node propagates vulnerability across the broader redox system → [Packer et al. (1997)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#packer_vitamin_1997) [1]

#### (Boundaries of the mechanism)

Transcriptional Nrf2 induction belongs to [BRS3(PM2) - Nrf2-ARE Antioxidant Activation](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/pm/brs3-pm2-nrf2-are-antioxidant-activation). Net ROS generation versus clearance is handled by [BRS3(PM3) - ROS Generation vs Clearance Balance](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/pm/brs3-pm3-ros-generation-vs-clearance-balance). Membrane lipid peroxidation control is represented by [BRS3(PM5) - Lipid Peroxidation Control](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/pm/brs3-pm5-lipid-peroxidation-control).

#### (Integration within BRS3)

This PM encodes network persistence within [BRS3(FM2)](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/fm/brs3-fm2-antioxidant-defense-capacity), drawing on [BRS3(KC1) - Antioxidant Substrate Availability](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/kc/brs3-kc1-antioxidant-substrate-availability). Mitochondrial electron-transport context imported through [BRS4(PM1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs4/pm/brs4-pm1-electron-transport-chain-function) modulates oxidative regeneration load.

</details>

### 4.1 Evidence Highlights

#### Introduction/Summary

The antioxidant-network concept is well established. The studies below anchor the regeneration logic that distinguishes network coverage from single-nutrient antioxidant framing.

<details>
<summary><strong>Evidence highlights — antioxidant network recycling</strong></summary>

- Vitamin E, vitamin C, glutathione, and CoQ10 function as an interacting regeneration network in vivo rather than independent antioxidants → [Packer et al. (1997)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#packer_vitamin_1997) [1]
- Network antioxidants display synergistic protection when combined, supporting mixed-coverage dietary patterns over megadose single-nutrient logic → [Vertuani et al. (2004)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#vertuani_antioxidants_2004) [2]

</details>
