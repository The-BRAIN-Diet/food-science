## BRS3(PM2) - Nrf2-ARE Antioxidant Activation

## 1. Definition

Activation of Nrf2-dependent antioxidant and detoxification gene programs that raise endogenous cellular protection.

This PM represents a route by which diet influences endogenous defence capacity, not just exogenous antioxidant supply, by helping induce gene programs involved in cellular protection and detoxification [1].

## 2. Intervention Breakdown

Food-State Dominant

## 3. Functional Role

↑ endogenous antioxidant gene expression

## 4. Mechanistic Basis

### Summary

BRS3(PM2) links crucifer-rich food states and cofactor sufficiency to increased endogenous antioxidant and detoxification responses within [BRS3(FM2) - Antioxidant Defense Capacity](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/fm/brs3-fm2-antioxidant-defense-capacity) [1].

<details>
<summary><strong>Nrf2 activation and endogenous antioxidant defence</strong></summary>

#### (Nrf2-ARE signalling)

Nrf2 activation increases transcription of cellular defence genes involved in antioxidant protection, redox buffering, and detoxification.

#### (Crucifer and sulforaphane context)

Broccoli, broccoli sprouts, and related crucifer foods provide glucoraphanin-derived sulforaphane signals that are especially relevant to this PM, with repeated intake patterns likely more meaningful than isolated exposures [1].

#### (KC dependence)

Because induced antioxidant programmes still require adequate substrate and cofactor context, this PM depends on both antioxidant-substrate and trace-mineral sufficiency listed in section 5.2.

</details>
