### BRS3(KC3) - Essential Fatty Acid Balance

### 1. Definition

Helps ensure availability and balance of omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids required for inflammatory signalling, membrane composition, and inflammation-resolution pathways.

* Constrains eicosanoid and SPM balance when omega-3/omega-6 ratio is unfavourable — within BRS3.
* Supports membrane composition relevant to neuronal signalling — Supporting BRS1.
* Underpins inflammation-resolution capacity in BRS3(FM3) — within BRS3.



### 2. Constraint Role

Provides the shared structural and signalling lipid pool required for inflammatory mediator production, membrane function, and inflammation-resolution pathways. Supports multiple inflammatory PMs through effects on membrane composition and lipid-derived signalling molecules.

### 3. Shared Biological Pool

- EPA
- DHA
- Arachidonic acid
- Omega-3 fatty acids
- Omega-6 fatty acids

### 4. Biological Importance

Essential fatty acids act as structural membrane components and as precursors for signalling molecules involved in inflammatory activation and resolution. Imbalances in fatty-acid availability may alter inflammatory tone and affect the efficiency of resolution pathways.
