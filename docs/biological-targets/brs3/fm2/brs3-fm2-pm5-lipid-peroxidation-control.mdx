## BRS3-FM2-PM5 - Lipid Peroxidation Control

## 1. Definition

Protects membrane lipids and polyunsaturated fatty acids from oxidative degradation — where antioxidant availability and lipid quality jointly determine whether vulnerable fatty acids are protected or damaged.

* Protects polyunsaturated fatty acids from oxidative membrane damage — within BRS3.
* Depends on antioxidant network recycling and substrate sufficiency — within BRS3.
* Intersects neuronal membrane lipid chemistry in BRS1 — Supporting BRS1.


## 2. Primary Biological Effects
↓ lipid peroxidation; ↑ membrane stability

## 5. Mechanistic Basis

### Summary

Polyunsaturated fatty acids in membranes are especially vulnerable to oxidative attack. When peroxidation outpaces lipid-phase antioxidant protection, structural and signalling lipid pools destabilise within [BRS3(FM2) - Antioxidant Defense Capacity](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/fm2/brs3-fm2-antioxidant-defense-capacity).

<details>
<summary><strong>Membrane protection and lipid peroxidation control</strong></summary>

#### (Lipid peroxidation burden)

Reactive oxidative load can propagate through membrane lipids, especially polyunsaturated fatty acids, generating damaged lipid species and secondary oxidative pressure → [Bulut et al., 2007]

#### (Lipid-phase antioxidant protection)

Vitamin E, carotenoids, and related lipid-phase antioxidants interrupt peroxidation chain reactions in membranes. Protection is matrix-dependent: antioxidant coverage must be present where vulnerable PUFAs are concentrated → [Fielding et al., 2005]; [Johnson, 2014]

#### (Boundaries of the mechanism)

Net ROS generation versus clearance is handled by [BRS3-FM2-PM4 - ROS Generation vs Clearance Balance](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/fm2/brs3-fm2-pm4-ros-generation-vs-clearance-balance). Transcriptional Nrf2 induction belongs to [BRS3-FM2-PM3 - Nrf2-ARE Antioxidant Activation](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/fm2/brs3-fm2-pm3-nrf2-are-antioxidant-activation). Eicosanoid and SPM substrate quality downstream is represented by [BRS3-FM3-PM8 - Eicosanoid / SPM Balance](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/fm3/brs3-fm3-pm8-eicosanoid-spm-balance).

#### (Integration within BRS3)

This PM protects membrane lipid integrity within [BRS3(FM2)](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/fm2/brs3-fm2-antioxidant-defense-capacity), drawing on [BRS3(KC1) - Antioxidant Substrate Availability](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/kc/brs3-kc1-antioxidant-substrate-availability) and [BRS3(KC3) - Essential Fatty Acid Balance](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/kc/brs3-kc3-essential-fatty-acid-balance). Mitochondrial redox context imported through [BRS4-FM2-PM4](/docs/biological-targets/brs4/fm2/brs4-fm2-pm4-ros-production-and-control) modulates generation load on membrane lipids.

</details>





### 5.1 Evidence Highlights

#### Introduction/Summary

Membrane lipid peroxidation biology is well established. The studies below highlight food-based protection patterns that refine how lipid-phase oxidative damage is interpreted.

<details>
<summary><strong>Dietary antioxidant protection of lipids</strong></summary>

- **Confidence:** low-medium
- **Evidence Level:** mechanistic
- **Rationale:** Antioxidant interventions can increase resistance to exercise-induced lipid peroxidation, supporting modifiable lipid-phase protection through food-state antioxidant coverage rather than isolated compounds [Fielding et al., 2005].
- **Key References:**

<PhenomeBibLinks items={[{"href":"/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#fielding_increases_2005","label":"Fielding et al. (2005)","dataLevel":"Human Study"}]} />

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Carotenoids and neural membrane protection</strong></summary>

- **Confidence:** low-medium
- **Evidence Level:** mechanistic
- **Rationale:** Carotenoids accumulate in neural tissues, scavenge reactive oxygen species, and play a neuroprotective role through antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties — supporting paired antioxidant–PUFA meal construction for this PM [Johnson, 2014].
- **Key References:**

<PhenomeBibLinks items={[{"href":"/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#johnson_role_2014","label":"Johnson (2014)","dataLevel":"Mechanistic"}]} />

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Oxidative damage and mitochondrial overlap</strong></summary>

- **Confidence:** low-medium
- **Evidence Level:** mechanistic
- **Rationale:** Increased oxidative stress links to cellular damage, DNA repair dysfunction, and mitochondrial impairment — placing lipid peroxidation control within wider energetic and inflammatory biology [Solleiro-Villavicencio & Rivas-Arancibia, 2018].
- **Key References:**
