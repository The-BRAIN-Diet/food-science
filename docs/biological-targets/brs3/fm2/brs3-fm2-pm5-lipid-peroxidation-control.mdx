## BRS3-FM2-PM5 - Lipid Peroxidation Control

## 1. Definition

Protection of membrane lipids and polyunsaturated fatty acids from oxidative degradation.

This PM governs oxidative damage control at the membrane level, where antioxidant availability and lipid quality jointly determine whether vulnerable fatty acids are protected or degraded.

## 2. Primary Biological Effects
↓ lipid peroxidation; ↑ membrane stability

## 3. Phenome Connections

## 5. Mechanistic Basis

### Summary

Polyunsaturated fatty acids in membranes are especially vulnerable to oxidative attack. When peroxidation outpaces lipid-phase antioxidant protection, structural and signalling lipid pools destabilise within [BRS3(FM2) - Antioxidant Defense Capacity](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/fm2/brs3-fm2-antioxidant-defense-capacity).

<details>
<summary><strong>Membrane protection and lipid peroxidation control</strong></summary>

#### (Lipid peroxidation burden)

Reactive oxidative load can propagate through membrane lipids, especially polyunsaturated fatty acids, generating damaged lipid species and secondary oxidative pressure → [Bulut et al., 2007] [Bulut et al., 2007]

#### (Lipid-phase antioxidant protection)

Vitamin E, carotenoids, and related lipid-phase antioxidants interrupt peroxidation chain reactions in membranes. Protection is matrix-dependent: antioxidant coverage must be present where vulnerable PUFAs are concentrated → [Fielding et al., 2005] [Fielding et al., 2005]; [Johnson, 2014] [Johnson, 2014]

#### (Boundaries of the mechanism)

Net ROS generation versus clearance is handled by [BRS3-FM2-PM6 - ROS Generation vs Clearance Balance](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/fm2/brs3-fm2-pm4-ros-generation-vs-clearance-balance). Transcriptional Nrf2 induction belongs to [BRS3-FM2-PM5 - Nrf2-ARE Antioxidant Activation](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/fm2/brs3-fm2-pm3-nrf2-are-antioxidant-activation). Eicosanoid and SPM substrate quality downstream is represented by [BRS3-FM3-PM8 - Eicosanoid / SPM Balance](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/fm3/brs3-fm3-pm8-eicosanoid-spm-balance).

#### (Integration within BRS3)

This PM protects membrane lipid integrity within [BRS3(FM2)](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/fm2/brs3-fm2-antioxidant-defense-capacity), drawing on [BRS3(KC1) - Antioxidant Substrate Availability](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/kc/brs3-kc1-antioxidant-substrate-availability) and [BRS3(KC3) - Essential Fatty Acid Balance](/docs/biological-targets/brs3/kc/brs3-kc3-essential-fatty-acid-balance). Mitochondrial redox context imported through [BRS4-FM2-PM4](/docs/biological-targets/brs4/fm2/brs4-fm2-pm4-ros-production-and-control) modulates generation load on membrane lipids.

</details>

### 5.1 Evidence Highlights

#### Introduction/Summary

Lipid peroxidation biology is well established. The studies below highlight biomarker and antioxidant-intervention findings that refine how membrane protection is interpreted in practice.

<details>
<summary><strong>Evidence highlights — membrane lipid protection</strong></summary>

- Malondialdehyde and related markers track lipid-peroxidation burden when oxidative generation exceeds clearance → [Bulut et al., 2007] [Bulut et al., 2007]
- Antioxidant interventions can increase resistance to exercise-induced lipid peroxidation, supporting modifiable lipid-phase protection → [Fielding et al., 2005] [Fielding et al., 2005]
- Carotenoid and vitamin E roles in limiting PUFA oxidation support paired antioxidant–marine-fat meal construction rather than isolated nutrient logic → [Johnson, 2014] [Johnson, 2014]

</details>
