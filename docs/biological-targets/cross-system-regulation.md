## BRS-X - Cross-System Regulation

## Overview

Biological Regulatory Systems (BRSs) describe the primary domains of biological regulation targeted by the BRAIN Framework. However, some biological systems operate across multiple BRSs simultaneously and cannot be accurately represented within a single domain.

**BRS-X** systems capture these cross-system regulatory networks. They function as higher-order signalling architectures that influence multiple BRSs concurrently and provide important integration layers within the wider framework.

Current BRS-X systems include:

- **Endocannabinoid System (ECS)**
- **Hormone Signalling & Regulation**

Additional BRS-X systems may be introduced in the future where sufficient evidence supports a genuinely cross-system biological role.

---

## BRS-X Systems

### [BRS-X(ECS) — Endocannabinoid System](/docs/biological-targets/brs-x/ecs/brs-x-ecs)

Cross-system signalling network involved in neurotransmission, inflammation, metabolism, mitochondrial regulation, gut–brain communication, appetite regulation, stress adaptation, and neuroplasticity.

Connected BRSs: BRS1, BRS3, BRS4, BRS5, BRS6.

### [BRS-X(Hormones) — Hormone Signalling & Regulation](/docs/biological-targets/brs-x/hormones/brs-x-hormones)

Cross-system endocrine signalling network governing hormone production, hormone-responsive regulation, microbial hormone metabolism, hormone recycling, and neuroendocrine communication across multiple biological systems.

Scope includes estrogen, progesterone, and testosterone signalling; hormone–microbiome interactions; estrobolome and androbolome biology; and hormonal adaptation across life stages.

Connected BRSs: BRS1, BRS2, BRS3, BRS4, BRS5, BRS6.

---

## Naming Convention

Identifiers use the **BRS-X** namespace:

- `BRS-X(ECS)`, `BRS-X(Hormones)`
- `BRS-X(ECS-FM1)`, `BRS-X(ECS-PM2)`, `BRS-X(Hormones-FM2)`, `BRS-X(Hormones-PM3)`

BRS-X mechanisms appear in **Connected Mechanisms** sections alongside standard BRS mechanisms — users do not need to distinguish namespace at the point of use.

---

## Long-Term Vision

BRS1–BRS6 remain the core biological architecture. BRS-X systems capture major biological signalling networks that influence multiple BRSs simultaneously.

Potential future candidates may include circadian biology and other cross-system signalling architectures. Promotion to BRS-X status should require strong evidence that a system operates as a genuine cross-system biological regulator rather than a simple modifier or intervention.
