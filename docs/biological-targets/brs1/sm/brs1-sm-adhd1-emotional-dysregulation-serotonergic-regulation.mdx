## BRS1(SM-ADHD1) - Emotional Dysregulation & Serotonergic Regulation

## 1. Definition

Therapeutic-area overlay for attention-related contexts where emotional dysregulation may coexist with monoamine precursor and transport biology. This SM interprets how tryptophan/tyrosine supply, LNAA competition, and noradrenergic modulation — as defined in connected PMs — may influence serotonergic and catecholaminergic balance at the meal level. It does not assert biomarkers, diagnoses, or treatment efficacy for ADHD.

## 2. Intervention Breakdown

Food-State Leaning

**Category:** SM-ADHD  
**Use case:** Emotional dysregulation with attention-related monoamine context

## 3. Functional Role

↑ precursor and transport context for monoamine balance; ↑ meal-pattern stability for emotional regulation support; ↓ LNAA-competition destabilisation

## 4. Mechanistic Basis

### Summary

[BRS1(PM1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs1/pm/brs1-pm1-tyrosine-tryptophan-precursor-supply) supplies tyrosine and tryptophan for catecholamine and serotonin pathways; [BRS1(PM2)](/docs/biological-targets/brs1/pm/brs1-pm2-lat1-competitive-transport-modulation) and [BRS1(FM2)](/docs/biological-targets/brs1/fm/brs1-fm2-glycaemic-modulation-of-neurotransmitter-balance) shape LNAA partitioning bias; [BRS1(PM5)](/docs/biological-targets/brs1/pm/brs1-pm5-noradrenergic-signalling-attention-executive-modulation) links noradrenergic attention context. BRS1(SM-ADHD1) applies that stable biology to emotional-regulation interpretation without redefining PM mechanisms.

<details>
<summary><strong>Monoamine precursors, transport bias, and regulatory framing</strong></summary>

#### (Precursor supply — PM1 biology)

Catecholamine and serotonin synthesis depend on adequate dietary tyrosine and tryptophan from protein-rich meals. Insufficient precursor supply limits downstream monoamine context before transport and enzymatic steps → [Wurtman et al. (2003)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#wurtman_effects_2003) [1]

#### (LNAA competition and glycaemic coupling)

Large neutral amino acid transport at the blood–brain barrier is competitive; meal composition and insulin-mediated partitioning can bias tryptophan versus tyrosine entry, affecting serotonin versus catecholamine bias → [Fernstrom (2013)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#fernstrom_lnna_2013) [2]

#### (Attention and emotional regulation as application layer)

[BRS1(PM5)](/docs/biological-targets/brs1/pm/brs1-pm5-noradrenergic-signalling-attention-executive-modulation) provides noradrenergic attention/executive context within [BRS1(FM1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs1/fm/brs1-fm1-catecholaminergic-function). This SM connects those mechanisms to emotional dysregulation framing — supportive dietary context only, not pharmacologic substitution.

</details>

## 6. Dietary Levers

<details>
<summary><strong>Diet</strong></summary>

- Distributed protein-rich meals with completeness/balance → precursor and LNAA context per [BRS1(PM1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs1/pm/brs1-pm1-tyrosine-tryptophan-precursor-supply) and [BRS1(KC2)](/docs/biological-targets/brs1/kc/brs1-kc2-amino-acid-completeness-and-balance).
- Carbohydrate quality and meal sequencing where glycaemic response may bias LNAA transport → [BRS1(FM2)](/docs/biological-targets/brs1/fm/brs1-fm2-glycaemic-modulation-of-neurotransmitter-balance).
- Cofactor-dense whole foods supporting monoamine synthesis context (B6, iron, folate).

</details>

## 7. Lifestyle Levers

<details>
<summary><strong>Lifestyle</strong></summary>

- Regular meal timing may stabilise precursor availability and reduce volatile LNAA competition across the day.
- Sleep and stress recovery may modulate noradrenergic and autonomic context interacting with attention-related regulation.
- Physical activity patterns may influence catecholamine tone where relevant to connected FM1 biology.

</details>
