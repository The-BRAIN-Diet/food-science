### BRS1(KC1) - Amino Acid Quality & Competitive Balance

(Shared Amino-Acid Pool for Neurotransmitter Biology)

### 1. Ambition

Maintain amino-acid quality and competitive balance so precursor sufficiency and LNAA transport context remain stable for neurotransmitter biology.

### 2. Shared Biological Pool

- Essential amino acids (EAAs) ← eggs, fish, dairy, legumes
- Large neutral amino acids (LNAAs) ← meat, fish, dairy, soy
- Tryptophan ← turkey, eggs, pumpkin seeds
- Tyrosine ← cheese, soy, fish

### 3. Biological Importance

Incomplete or imbalanced essential amino-acid intake limits precursor supply and LNAA transport competitiveness [Fernstrom, 2013; Mariotti et al., 2019]. Protein-quality frameworks emphasise indispensable amino-acid sufficiency rather than total protein mass alone. When amino-acid quality remains weak, precursor adequacy and transport context can degrade before downstream signalling mechanisms are considered.
