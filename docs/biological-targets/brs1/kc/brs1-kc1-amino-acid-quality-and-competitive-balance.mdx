### BRS1(KC1) - Amino Acid Quality & Competitive Balance

(Shared Amino-Acid Resource for Neurotransmitter Biology)

### 1. Ambition

Maintain indispensable amino-acid adequacy and competitive LNAA balance so meal-level protein can support precursor sufficiency and fair blood–brain transport context across connected BRS1 neurotransmitter mechanisms.

### 2. Core Nutritional Requirements

- Essential amino acids (EAAs) ← eggs, fish, dairy, legumes
- Large neutral amino acids (LNAAs) ← meat, fish, dairy, soy
- Tryptophan ← eggs, turkey, pumpkin seeds
- Tyrosine ← cheese, soy, fish

### 3. Evidence Base

#### Summary

Connected BRS1 mechanisms share one upstream nutritional condition: the diet must supply **adequate indispensable amino acids**, and meal-level **LNAA balance** must allow relevant precursors to reach the brain without chronic competitive disadvantage. Protein-quality and distribution matter as much as total protein — assess indispensable amino-acid adequacy (including tryptophan and tyrosine within the broader EAA and LNAA pool), not protein mass alone.

Practical framing: distribute **complete or complementary protein** across meals; where plant proteins dominate, pair sources to cover the indispensable set; recognise that the same meal can affect both **pool sufficiency** and **relative LNAA competition** at the blood–brain barrier. The evidence claim here is **constraint prevention** — adequate availability is required to prevent biological constraint — not that additional intake enhances performance in nutrient-sufficient people.

#### Biological Importance

Essential amino acids are the foundational dietary requirement for this KC. They cannot be synthesised in adequate amounts endogenously and must be supplied through diet. Multiple connected BRS1 mechanisms — amino-acid availability, LAT1 transport, monoaminergic signalling, and protein-derived substrate context for excitatory–inhibitory biology — all assume a sufficiently complete indispensable amino-acid pool before transport bias or receptor-level signalling are considered.

#### Biological Importance

Large neutral amino acids share LAT1-mediated transport at the blood–brain barrier. Dietary patterns that alter plasma LNAA ratios can shift relative precursor delivery to the brain even when total protein intake appears adequate. This is why **competitive balance** is part of the KC alongside indispensable amino-acid sufficiency — meal composition can change which precursors are favourably presented for brain entry.

<h4 class="brs-hub-integration-section-title">Supporting Evidence</h4>

#### Biological Importance

Tryptophan and tyrosine are named separately in §2 because they are indispensable dietary precursors and major LNAA competitors with distinct roles across connected monoaminergic biology — tryptophan for serotonergic precursor context, tyrosine for catecholamine precursor context. Tyrosine can also be formed from phenylalanine when phenylalanine status is adequate; dietary tyrosine still matters because it directly contributes to precursor supply and LAT1 competition at meals.

<h4 class="brs-hub-integration-section-title">Supporting Evidence</h4>
<ul class="brs-hub-integration-evidence-list">
<li class="brs-hub-integration-evidence-item"><p><a href="/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#fernstrom_lnna_2013">Fernstrom, 2013</a> — Established that tryptophan brain entry competes with other large neutral amino acids at shared transport sites, linking meal-level LNAA patterning to serotonergic precursor availability — supporting the KC requirement to name tryptophan as a distinct precursor constraint within the shared pool.</p></li>
<li class="brs-hub-integration-evidence-item"><p><a href="/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#fernstrom_lnna_2013">Fernstrom, 2013</a> — Demonstrated that dietary tyrosine availability and LNAA competition jointly influence catecholamine-relevant precursor presentation at the blood–brain barrier — supporting the KC requirement to name tyrosine as a distinct catecholamine precursor constraint alongside broader EAA and LNAA adequacy.</p></li>
</ul>

### 4. Emerging Biological Supports

No Emerging Biological Supports are currently prioritised for this KC.

Plausible enhancers such as taurine, creatine, or conditionally useful amino-acid derivatives may support related regulatory capacities, but they are not established as shared indispensable requirements for amino-acid quality and LNAA competitive balance. Where evidence becomes source-led and KC-specific, candidates can be added here without blurring the Core Nutritional Requirements boundary.
