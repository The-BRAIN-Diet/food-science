### BRS1(KC1) - Amino Acid Substrate Sufficiency

### 1. Definition

Availability of meaningful dietary amino-acid substrate at the meal level to support brain-relevant amino-acid biology.

Maintenance of excitatory–inhibitory stability depends not only on substrate availability, but also on energetically and enzymatically maintained neurotransmitter cycling, transporter activity, receptor regulation, antioxidant defence, and membrane integrity. This Key Constraint therefore contributes to the broader endogenous regulatory capacity required to sustain stable neurotransmission under varying physiological demands.

Also supporting glutamine/glutamate substrate context relevant to excitatory–inhibitory neurotransmitter cycling.

### 2. Constraint Role

Provides the baseline dietary amino-acid substrate pool required for large neutral amino acids and other meal-level amino-acid inputs that downstream BRS1 mechanisms depend on [1][2]. Enables dependent PMs and FMs when protein and amino-acid intake at meals is sufficient to support normal amino-acid availability—not when specific aromatic precursors are singled out as isolated targets.

Completeness, balance, and transport bias are handled by [BRS1(KC2)](/docs/biological-targets/brs1/kc/brs1-kc2-amino-acid-completeness-and-balance) and [BRS1(PM2)](/docs/biological-targets/brs1/pm/brs1-pm2-lat1-competitive-transport-modulation) respectively.

### 3. Supporting Inputs/Substrates

- Complementary amino-acid combinations where single sources are limiting
- Dietary protein
- Glutamine/glutamate
- Large neutral amino acids (LNAAs) at the meal level

### 4. Biological Importance

Brain-relevant neurotransmitter biology depends on whether meals deliver enough dietary amino-acid substrate to sustain the amino-acid pool used by transport, synthesis, and clearance pathways [1][2]. Inadequate protein intake or poor distribution across the day can weaken substrate sufficiency before completeness, prioritisation, or competitive transport are considered.

### 6. Constraint Stressors / Burdens

- Low-protein meals or inadequate protein distribution across the day [2]
- Amino-acid-poor protein sources without compensating combinations
- Chronic energy deficit reducing overall amino-acid intake
- Erratic meal timing limiting consistent amino-acid availability
- Very low indispensable amino-acid intake weakening baseline substrate sufficiency [2]
