## BRS-X(Hormones-PM2) - Estrobolome Regulation

## 1. Definition

Microbiome-mediated regulation of oestrogen metabolism, deconjugation, recycling, and elimination through beta-glucuronidase activity and enterohepatic circulation.

## 2. Functional Role

↑ balanced oestrogen deconjugation/recycling context; ↓ dysbiotic beta-glucuronidase skew; ↑ enterohepatic regulation of sex-hormone exposure

## 3. Target Functional Outcome / Phenome

These mappings are translational relationships, not single-mechanism outcome claims. Phenomes are emergent functional patterns supported by multiple interacting PMs across the BRAIN Framework.

## 5. Mechanistic Basis

### Summary

The estrobolome links microbial enzyme activity to systemic oestrogen exposure through enterohepatic circulation, constrained by fermentable substrate availability via [BRS5(KC1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs5/kc/brs5-kc1-fermentable-fibre-availability) [1][2][3].

<details>
<summary><strong>Estrobolome, beta-glucuronidase, and enterohepatic recycling</strong></summary>

#### (Microbial oestrogen metabolism)

Gut bacteria with beta-glucuronidase activity can deconjugate oestrogen metabolites in the intestinal lumen, influencing whether oestrogens are recycled via enterohepatic circulation or eliminated → [Hu et al. (2023)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#hu_gut_microbial_beta_glucuronidase_estrogen_2023) [1]; [Sui et al. (2021)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#sui_gut_microbial_beta_glucuronidase_estrogen_reactivation_2021) [2]; [Ervin et al. (2019)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#ervin_estrobolome_beta_glucuronidase_2019) [3]

#### (Substrate dependence)

Fermentable fibre and diverse plant substrates support microbial ecology that may favour more balanced estrobolome function relative to low-fibre, ultra-processed dietary patterns.

#### (Boundaries of the mechanism)

Direct neural oestrogen signalling is owned by [BRS-X(Hormones-PM1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs-x/hormones/fm1/brs-x-hormones-pm1-oestrogen-signalling-stability). Barrier containment and LPS context intersect via [BRS5(FM1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs5/fm1/brs5-fm1-gut-barrier-integrity-and-immune-interface).

#### (Integration within BRS-X(Hormones))

This PM operationalises the gut-mediated oestrogen recycling arm of BRS-X(Hormones-FM1), dependent on [BRS5(KC1) — Fermentable Fibre Availability](/docs/biological-targets/brs5/kc/brs5-kc1-fermentable-fibre-availability).

</details>
