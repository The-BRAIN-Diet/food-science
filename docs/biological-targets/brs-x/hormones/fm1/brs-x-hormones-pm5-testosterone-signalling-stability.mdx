## BRS-X(Hormones-PM5) - Testosterone Signalling Stability

## 1. Definition

Integrated regulation of testosterone availability and androgen receptor signalling influencing behavioural activation, motivation, mental stamina, persistence, and goal-directed effort.

Sex hormones influence emotional and cognitive processing through widespread neural effects; testosterone intersects dopaminergic motivation circuits, effort allocation, and fatigue vulnerability where androgen exposure is low [Celec et al. (2015)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#celec_testosterone_brain_behavioral_functions_2015) [1].

## 2. Functional Role

↑ behavioural activation; ↑ motivation; ↑ persistence; ↑ mental stamina; ↑ goal-directed effort; ↓ fatigue vulnerability where low testosterone contributes

## 5. Mechanistic Basis

### Summary

Testosterone availability and androgen receptor signalling intersect dopaminergic activation, cellular energy context, and stress-axis allocation within [BRS-X(Hormones-FM1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs-x/hormones/fm1/brs-x-hormones-fm1-reproductive-hormone-balance-and-neurocognitive-regulation), linking BRS1 catecholaminergic drive with BRS4 bioenergetic capacity and BRS6 HPA stress mechanisms [1][2].

<details>
<summary><strong>Androgen signalling and behavioural activation</strong></summary>

#### (Motivation and effort allocation)

Testosterone influences brain behavioural functions including motivation, persistence, and goal-directed effort through androgen receptor signalling and dopaminergic interface context → [Celec et al. (2015)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#celec_testosterone_brain_behavioral_functions_2015) [1]

#### (Symptomatic energy and stamina context)

Meta-analytic evidence associates testosterone treatment with symptomatic improvements in energy, mood, and quality-of-life domains in androgen-deficient subgroups → [Hackett et al. (2023)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#hackett_symptomatic_benefits_testosterone_treatment_2023) [2]

#### (Boundaries of the mechanism)

Microbial androgen metabolism belongs to [BRS-X(Hormones-PM6) — Androgen-Microbiome Regulation](/docs/biological-targets/brs-x/hormones/fm1/brs-x-hormones-pm6-androgen-microbiome-regulation). Male ageing, low testosterone states, gender-affirming hormone therapy, and chronic stress-related androgen suppression are Biological / Life-Stage Contexts rather than mechanisms on this page.

#### (Integration within BRS-X(Hormones))

This PM operationalises the direct androgen-signalling arm of BRS-X(Hormones-FM1), with cross-links to BRS1 dopaminergic mechanisms, BRS4 cellular bioenergetics, and BRS6 stress and HPA regulation.

</details>
