### BRS-X(ECS-KC1) - Phospholipid & NAPE Precursor Sufficiency

(Food Fats That Feed Brain Lipid Signalling)

### 1. Ambition

Maintain phospholipid and NAPE precursor sufficiency so endocannabinoidome lipid signalling capacity can be sustained through endogenous biosynthesis.

### 2. Core Nutritional Requirements

- Phosphatidylethanolamine-rich inputs ← eggs, fish roe, liver
- Phospholipid-rich whole-food matrix ← oats, legumes, fish
- Choline-linked precursor pool ← eggs, liver, soy

### 3. Evidence Base

#### Summary

Connected BRS-X(ECS) mechanisms share one upstream nutritional condition: the diet must supply **phospholipid and NAPE precursor sufficiency** — phosphatidylethanolamine-rich inputs, whole-food phospholipid matrices, and choline-linked precursors — sufficient to sustain endogenous N-acyl ethanolamine biosynthesis without pharmacological CB1 or CB2 targeting. This is the strongest dietary entry point into endocannabinoidome biology as a shared substrate pool.

Practical framing: include **eggs, fish roe, oily fish, oats, legumes, and choline-rich whole foods** across meals; recognise that NAPE→NAE biosynthesis and omega-3-derived endocannabinoidome signalling both assume adequate phospholipid precursor availability. The evidence claim here is **constraint prevention** — adequate availability is required to prevent biological constraint — not that additional intake enhances performance in nutrient-sufficient people.

#### Biological Importance

Phosphatidylethanolamine is the direct membrane-phospholipid precursor for NAPE formation — the entry substrate for endogenous N-acyl ethanolamine biosynthesis represented in ECS-PM1. Phospholipid-rich whole-food matrices in oats, legumes, and fish supply the broader dietary context in which NAPE precursor availability is maintained across connected ECS FM1 mechanisms.

#### Biological Importance

Choline-linked precursors support phosphatidylcholine and phosphatidylethanolamine interconversion within membrane phospholipid pools that feed NAPE biosynthesis and intersect omega-3-derived endocannabinoidome signalling on ECS-PM2. Dietary choline therefore belongs in the shared KC pool as a membrane-precursor class rather than an isolated PM cofactor.

### 4. Emerging Biological Supports

No Emerging Biological Supports are currently prioritised for this KC.

Phospholipid supplements, phosphatidylserine, or direct NAE precursors may support related neuromodulatory capacities under specific conditions, but they are not established as shared indispensable dietary requirements for phospholipid and NAPE precursor sufficiency. Where evidence becomes source-led and KC-specific, candidates can be added here without blurring the Core Nutritional Requirements boundary.
