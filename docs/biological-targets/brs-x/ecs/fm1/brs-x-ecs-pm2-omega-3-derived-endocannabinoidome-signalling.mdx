## BRS-X(ECS-PM2) - Omega-3-Derived Endocannabinoidome Signalling

## 1. Definition

Production of omega-3-derived ethanolamides and related signalling molecules including DHEA and EPEA from EPA and DHA availability.

## 2. Functional Role

↑ omega-3-derived ethanolamide signalling; ↑ anti-inflammatory endocannabinoidome context; ↑ neuroprotective and neurogenesis-supportive lipid mediator pools; ↓ low EPA/DHA substrate limitation

## 3. Target Functional Outcome / Phenome

## 5. Mechanistic Basis

### Summary

EPA and DHA availability supports production of omega-3-derived ethanolamides including DHEA and EPEA within [BRS-X(ECS-FM1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs-x/ecs/fm1/brs-x-ecs-fm1-endocannabinoidome-signalling-capacity-and-neuromodulatory-regulation), linking dietary omega-3 biology to endocannabinoidome signalling rather than CB1 receptor activation [1][2][3].

<details>
<summary><strong>Omega-3 ethanolamide signalling</strong></summary>

#### (DHEA and EPEA production)

Emerging evidence supports omega-3 fatty acid-derived endocannabinoids and ethanolamide derivatives as diet-actionable signalling molecules with neurobiological relevance → [Watson et al. (2019)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#watson_emerging_2019) [1]

#### (Omega-3 and endocannabinoid system coupling)

Omega-3 fatty acids intersect mechanistically with endocannabinoid system tone and cardiometabolic-inflammatory context → [Saleh-Ghadimi et al. (2020)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#saleh-ghadimi_endocannabinoid_2020) [2]

#### (DHEA immunomodulatory signalling)

DHA-derived docosahexaenoylethanolamine (DHEA) shows anti-inflammatory N-acyl ethanolamide activity in macrophage models, supporting a diet-linked omega-3 endocannabinoidome mechanism → [Meijerink et al. (2011)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#meijerink_docosahexaenoylethanolamine_2011) [3]

#### (Boundaries of the mechanism)

Classical NAPE → NAE biosynthesis from phospholipids belongs to [BRS-X(ECS-PM1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs-x/ecs/fm1/brs-x-ecs-pm1-nape-nae-biosynthesis-capacity). Membrane DHA incorporation belongs to [BRS1-FM4-PM6](/docs/biological-targets/brs1/fm4/brs1-fm4-pm6-neuronal-membrane-dha-incorporation).

#### (Integration within BRS-X(ECS))

This PM operationalises the omega-3 arm of BRS-X(ECS-FM1), intersecting [BRS-X(ECS-KC1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs-x/ecs/kc/brs-x-ecs-kc1-phospholipid-nape-precursor-availability) phospholipid substrate context.

</details>
