## BRS-X(ECS-PM3) - FAAH-Mediated Endocannabinoid Preservation

## 1. Definition

Regulation of fatty acid amide hydrolase (FAAH) activity influencing the persistence and degradation of anandamide and related NAEs.

## 2. Functional Role

↑ endogenous NAE persistence; ↑ FAAH-sensitive tone preservation; ↓ accelerated anandamide degradation where diet-sensitive FAAH modulation applies

## 3. Target Functional Outcome / Phenome

## 5. Mechanistic Basis

### Summary

FAAH represents a plausible diet-sensitive amplification point for preserving endogenous anandamide and related NAE signalling within [BRS-X(ECS-FM1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs-x/ecs/fm1/brs-x-ecs-fm1-endocannabinoidome-signalling-capacity-and-neuromodulatory-regulation), focusing on endogenous tone preservation rather than CB1/CB2 receptor stimulation [1][2].

<details>
<summary><strong>FAAH and endogenous tone preservation</strong></summary>

#### (Polyphenol–FAAH interface)

Genistein and related polyphenols may inhibit cellular anandamide uptake and FAAH-linked degradation pathways → [Thors et al. (2007)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#thors_inhibition_2007) [1]

#### (FAAH and dopaminergic reward context)

FAAH inhibition may prolong anandamide effects on mesolimbic dopamine signalling, supporting reward-related neuromodulatory context → [Solinas et al. (2006)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#solinas_anandamide_2006) [2]

#### (Boundaries of the mechanism)

This PM addresses degradation and preservation of endogenous NAEs, not NAPE biosynthesis ([BRS-X(ECS-PM1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs-x/ecs/fm1/brs-x-ecs-pm1-nape-nae-biosynthesis-capacity)) or direct dopamine production ([BRS1(FM1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs1/fm1/brs1-fm1-monoaminergic-function)).

#### (Integration within BRS-X(ECS))

This PM operationalises the FAAH-preservation arm of BRS-X(ECS-FM1), complementing biosynthesis and omega-3 ethanolamide PMs.

</details>
