## BRS2(SM-SNP1) - SNP-Sensitive Methylation Efficiency

## 1. Definition

Helps explain why genetic variation in MTHFR, COMT, and BDNF may alter methylation efficiency and methyl-donor responsiveness. Variant-sensitive reading adds context without redefining core BRS2 mechanism biology.

* Modulates how folate remethylation and SAMe support are interpreted — within BRS2.
* May alter sensitivity of monoaminergic and catecholamine pathways to diet — Supporting BRS1.
* Influences how methylation strain connects to stress and cognitive contexts — Supporting BRS3.

## 2. Phenome Connections

These mappings are translational relationships, not single-mechanism outcome claims. Phenomes are emergent functional patterns supported by multiple interacting PMs across the BRAIN Framework.

No direct functional outcome relationship currently mapped.

## 3. Intervention Breakdown

Food-State Leaning

## 4. Primary Biological Effects

↑ methylation-efficiency support; ↑ cofactor sufficiency awareness; ↑ downstream neurochemical resilience support

## 5. Mechanistic Basis

### Summary

Variant-sensitive efficiency can shift donor and cofactor responsiveness under the same remethylation and SAMe biology ([BRS2(KC1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs2/kc/brs2-kc1-one-carbon-donor-pool), [BRS2-FM1-PM1](/docs/biological-targets/brs2/fm1/brs2-fm1-pm1-folate-b12-dependent-homocysteine-remethylation), [BRS2-FM1-PM3](/docs/biological-targets/brs2/fm1/brs2-fm1-pm3-same-synthesis), [BRS2(FM1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs2/fm1/brs2-fm1-methylation-cycle-efficiency)). Outcomes vary by genotype and diet pattern; BRS2(SM-SNP1) explains why that variability matters without deterministic SNP outcome claims.

<details>
<summary><strong>Variant-sensitive pathways</strong></summary>

- **MTHFR** — folate, B2, and B12 sufficiency weigh more when folate-pathway efficiency is reduced [Aragão et al., 2024]
- **COMT** — catecholamine clearance may interact with methylation-adjacent meal patterns; see [BRS1(SM-SNP1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs1/sm/brs1-sm-snp1-comt-catecholamine-clearance-sensitivity) for BRS1 clearance framing [Hünnerkopf et al., 2007]
- **BDNF Val66Met** — methionine–homocysteine and plasticity relevance downstream [Hünnerkopf et al., 2007]
- **Omega-3 / polyphenols** — membrane and resilience support alongside one-carbon inputs [Ziaei et al., 2024]

</details>

<summary><strong>Diet</strong></summary>

- B2 ← eggs, dairy, leafy greens

- Folate ← leafy greens, legumes, liver
