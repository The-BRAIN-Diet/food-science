## BRS2(S1) - SNP-Sensitive Methylation Efficiency

## 1. Definition

Genetic modifiers alter one-carbon pathway efficiency rather than creating a standalone mechanism. This overlay includes MTHFR-related methyl-donor utilisation, COMT cross-talk with catecholamine clearance context, and BDNF Val66Met downstream relevance for variable neurochemical response. It is a modifier layer and personalization node for BRSX — not a replacement for [BRS2(PM1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs2/pm/brs2-pm1-folate-b12-dependent-homocysteine-remethylation) or [BRS2(PM3)](/docs/biological-targets/brs2/pm/brs2-pm3-same-synthesis).

## 2. Intervention Breakdown

Food-State Leaning

**Category:** SM-SNP  
**Use case:** Genetic modifiers (MTHFR, COMT cross-talk, BDNF Val66Met) altering one-carbon pathway efficiency

## 3. Functional Role

↑ genotype-aware methyl-donor and cofactor context; ↑ variable methylation-efficiency interpretation; ↑ downstream neurochemical-response modulation context (non-deterministic)

## 4. Mechanistic Basis

### Summary

[BRS2(KC1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs2/kc/brs2-kc1-methyl-donor-sufficiency), [BRS2(PM1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs2/pm/brs2-pm1-folate-b12-dependent-homocysteine-remethylation), and [BRS2(PM3)](/docs/biological-targets/brs2/pm/brs2-pm3-same-synthesis) define stable remethylation and SAMe biology within [BRS2(FM1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs2/fm/brs2-fm1-methylation-cycle-efficiency). BRS2(S1) interprets how SNP-sensitive efficiency (MTHFR, COMT, BDNF Val66Met) may shift the *same* dietary inputs toward variable methylation capacity and downstream neurochemical response — without deterministic genotype outcomes.

<details>
<summary><strong>SNP-sensitive efficiency, cross-BRS modifiers, and connected PM biology</strong></summary>

#### (MTHFR and methyl-donor context — PM1 / KC1)

Reduced folate-pathway efficiency increases reliance on food-state folate, B12, and riboflavin (B2) cofactor context alongside [BRS2(KC1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs2/kc/brs2-kc1-methyl-donor-sufficiency). Contemporary one-carbon reviews emphasise integrated donor and cofactor patterns rather than isolated SNP narratives → [Aragão et al. (2024)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#aragao_revitalising_2024) [1]

#### (SAMe synthesis — PM3)

[BRS2(PM3)](/docs/biological-targets/brs2/pm/brs2-pm3-same-synthesis) remains the authoritative SAMe synthesis mechanism; variant-sensitive efficiency changes how much upstream remethylation stress is felt at the SAMe node, not the core PM definition.

#### (COMT cross-talk and BDNF Val66Met)

COMT-related catecholamine clearance may interact with methylation-adjacent meal patterns (BRS1 cross-talk). BDNF Val66Met has been discussed in relation to methionine–homocysteine pathway interactions and downstream plasticity context → [Hünnerkopf et al. (2007)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#hunnerkopf_interaction_2007) [2]

#### (Omega-3 and BDNF-supportive dietary context)

Omega-3 status may modulate inflammatory and plasticity-related context alongside one-carbon patterns; systematic reviews address nutrient–genotype interaction frameworks without prescribing deterministic outcomes → [Ziaei et al. (2024)](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#ziaei_systematic_2024) [3]

#### (BRSX personalization node)

Cross-link BRSX: this SM is a personalization layer for genotype-sensitive interpretation across systems — it must not be read as a standalone PM or diagnostic rule.

</details>

## 6. Dietary Levers

<details>
<summary><strong>Diet</strong></summary>

- Riboflavin/folate foods → MTHFR pathway support context (B2 + methyl donors).
- Oily fish → omega-3 context for downstream neurochemical and plasticity framing.
- Polyphenol-rich foods → BDNF-supportive dietary context (non-deterministic).
- Daily, food-state patterns prioritising methyl-donor sufficiency per [BRS2(KC1)](/docs/biological-targets/brs2/kc/brs2-kc1-methyl-donor-sufficiency).

## 7. Lifestyle Levers

<details>
<summary><strong>Lifestyle</strong></summary>

- Avoid treating genotype as a prescription for high-dose single nutrients — pattern-level food-state emphasis only.

</details>
