### BRS2(KC2) - Methionine & Transsulfuration Substrate Pool

### 1. Ambition

Maintain sulfur amino-acid substrate sufficiency to support methionine-cycle throughput, transsulfuration, and glutathione-linked protection.

### 2. Shared Biological Pool

- Methionine ← eggs, fish, poultry
- Serine ← soy foods, eggs, turkey
- Glycine ← gelatin-rich cuts, poultry, fish
- Cysteine ← eggs, chicken, yogurt

### 3. Biological Importance

Methylation throughput and sulfur-redox protection draw from overlapping amino-acid resources rather than isolated pathways. The same substrate pool supports methionine regeneration, transsulfuration flow, and glutathione-linked protection, so insufficiency can constrain multiple PMs in parallel. This makes sulfur amino-acid sufficiency a shared stability requirement across BRS2.
