## Overview

Lycopene is a red carotenoid pigment found primarily in tomatoes and other red fruits and vegetables. It functions as a potent antioxidant, scavenging reactive oxygen species and helping stabilize cell membranes. Lycopene exhibits anti-inflammatory properties and supports immune regulation. Unlike some other carotenoids, lycopene's bioavailability is significantly enhanced by cooking, which breaks down cell walls and releases the compound. Its absorption is also improved when consumed with dietary fats, making food preparation and pairing important for maximizing benefits.
