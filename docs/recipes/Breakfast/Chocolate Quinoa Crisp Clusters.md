# chocolate quinoa crisp clusters

> A cereal-to-snack hybrid engineered with whole quinoa microstructure, flax mucilage binding, and solid-phase lipid crystallisation. Designed for steady energy, a low glycemic profile, and spoonable crunch.

## ü•£ Method

### 1. Cook & cool the quinoa

- Rinse quinoa thoroughly.

- Cook using **2:1 water : quinoa ratio** until tender.

- Drain well.

- Spread quinoa onto a tray and let it **air-dry 10‚Äì20 minutes**.

> This reduces surface moisture and preserves the grain's outer wall, which is key for cluster crunch.

---

### 2. Mix the dry ingredients

In a large bowl:

- quinoa (whole, **not blended**)

- oat flour  

- ground flax  

- cocoa/cacao  

- salt

Fold gently until distributed.

> Avoid mashing ‚Äî intact grains provide microstructure and aeration.

### 3. Add the wet binder

- Melt coconut oil (35‚Äì45 ¬∞C ‚Äî **not smoking**).

- Stir maple/date syrup + vanilla into the oil.

- Pour into the bowl and fold until coated.

> **The coconut oil coats the surface of the clusters and crystallises as it cools, forming a thin lipid shell that reduces moisture absorption and helps maintain the cereal-style crunch produced by dehydration and the preserved grain structure.**

> As the clusters cool, the lipid shell sets and the internal structure stabilises.

### 7. Puffed quinoa finish (optional)

- Place puffed quinoa in a bowl.

- Roll or pat the **cooled** clusters over the surface.

> This preserves the identity of puffed grains and prevents them from softening during baking.

## üß† Mechanistic Overview

### üî¨ Microstructure preservation

Whole quinoa grains provide:

- intact cell walls  

- air pocket formation  

- gentle surface dehydration  

‚Üí **primary source of crunch**

### üß¨ Flax mucilage

Hydrates and forms **gelatinous binding**, adding:

- viscosity  

- cluster cohesion  

- soluble fiber

### üç´ Cacao/cocoa

Provides:

- polyphenols  

- bitterness modulation  

- antioxidant capacity

### üßä Coconut oil crystallisation

As coconut oil cools:

- saturated lipids form semi-rigid crystals  

- a thin shell forms  

- moisture re-entry slows  

‚Üí **crunch is preserved**, not created by the fat itself.
