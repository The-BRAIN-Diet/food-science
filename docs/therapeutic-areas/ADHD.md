## Overview

ADHD shares overlapping biological dysfunctions with other neurocognitive conditions, notably chronic inflammation, neurochemical imbalance, mitochondrial impairment, oxidative stress, impaired methylation, gut–brain axis disruption, and glucose dysregulation (see [Tardy et al. 2020](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#tardy_vitamins_2020); [Mohamed and Kobeissy 2024](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#mohamed_nutrition_2024)). Targeted dietary trials—from restricted elimination diets to Mediterranean-leaning protocols—show modest yet reproducible symptom relief alongside cardiometabolic benefits (see [Stevenson et al. 2014](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#stevenson_research_2014); [Darabi et al. 2022](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#darabi_adherence_2022); [Aksoy and Doguer 2025](/docs/papers/BRAIN-Diet-References#aksoy_dietary_2025)).
